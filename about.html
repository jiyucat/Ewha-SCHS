What is the music therapy?

Music therapy is an evidence-based therapeutic approach that use music and music experiences to enhance the independence and quality of life for individuals with developmental and psychosocial needs. Music therapy benefits a wide range of populations, but for children and adolescents, it specifically aims to develop and enhance skills across physical, cognitive, language, social, and emotional domains. This process enables individuals in their developmental stages to maximize their potential abilities and adapt harmoniously to their environments, including home, school, and community. Grounded in scientific evidence, music therapy programs are tailored to meet each individual’s unique needs, systematically incorporating musical elements and fostering a therapist-client relationship that promotes growth.

About our music therapy training and education

SCHS-EWHA Music Therapy Training and Education program aims to train and empower local teachers and professionals in Sharjah and the broader Arab region with music therapy expertise, enabling them to integrate it into their fields of expertise and provide children and adolescents at SCHS with more professional and holistic support. The program not only provides traniees with a strong foundation in music therapy theory and knowledge, helping them understand its therapeutic applications, but also strengthens their practical and clinical skills in implementing music-based programs and incorporating music into other therapeutic approaches. Furthermore, it aims to develop trainee’s research competence in systematically managing the entire music therapy program and providing empirical evidence to support its process and progress.

Our Partnership with Ewha

Built on shared vision and commitment, our partnership with Ewha has expanded and strengthened since 0000.
We have closely collaborated in the areas of music therapy services, teacher training, and research initiatives.
Since the initial music therapy workshop held in 0000, we have jointly hosted an annual music therapy workshop.
Music therapy professionals, including interns and supervisors from Ewha, have provided music therapy services for children at SCHS.
Additionally, we have conducted teacher training programs, awarding certificates to teachers who have fulfilled both the training and clinical hour requirements.
Supervisors from Ewha have provided professional supervisions throughout the process.
Furthermore, international conferences have been held in collaboration with Ewha, and ongoing research efforts have been initiated to provide empirical evidence supporting the progress and effectiveness of music therapy.
Outcomes and insights have been shared through regular case presentations.
